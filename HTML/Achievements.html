DR. APJ ABDUL KALAM’S ACHIEVEMENTS

Dr.Kalam received 7 honorary doctorates from 40 universities.

The Government of India honoured Dr. kalam with the Padma Bhushan in 1981 and the Padma Vibhushan in 1990 for his work with ISRO and DRDO and his role as a scientific advisor to the Government.

In 1997, Dr. Kalam received India’s highest civilian honour, the Bharat Ratna, for his contribution to the scientific research and modernisation of defence technology in India.

In 2013, Dr. Kalam was the recipient of the “Von Braun Award from the National Space Society” to recognise excellence in the management and leadership of a space-related project”.

Tamil Nadu state government announced that Dr. Kalam’s birthday, 15 October, would be observed across the state as “Youth Renaissance Day;”

Tamil Nadu state government also instituted “Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam Award. The award is awarded annually on Independence Day, it began in 2015 for the residents of the state with achievements in promoting scientific growth, the humanities or the welfare of students.

Prime Minister Narendra Modi ceremonially released postage stamps commemorating Dr. Kalam at DRDO Bhawan in New Delhi on 15 October 2015 on the 84th anniversary of Dr. Kalam’s birth.

Researchers at the NASA’s Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL) discovered a new bacterium on the filters of the International Space Station (ISS) and named it Solibacillus kalamii to honour the late president Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam.

Kerala Technological University, headquartered at Thiruvananthapuram was renamed to A P J Abdul Kalam Technological University after his demise.

India’s First Medical Tech Institute named as Kalam Institute of Health Technology located at Visakhapatnam.

India and the US launched the Fulbright-Kalam Climate Fellowship in September 2014. The first call for applicants was announced on Friday, 12 March 2016, for the fellowship which enabled up to 6 Indian PhD students and post-doctoral researchers to work with US host institutions for a period of 6–12 months. The fellowship operates by the binational US-India Educational Foundation (USIEF) under the Fulbright programme.

Dr APJ Abdul Kalam Planetarium in Burla, Sambalpur, Odisha was named after him.
